# The Three Choices

---

# A / An / The / No Article

| Article | Use When... | Example |
|---------|------------|---------|
| **a / an** | Any one of a group (first mention) | "I saw **a** dog." |
| **the** | A specific one (both know which) | "**The** dog was brown." |
| **(none)** | General meaning, plural, uncountable | "**Dogs** are loyal." |

<div v-click class="mt-4 p-4 bg-yellow-50 rounded-lg">

**Key question:** Is the listener/reader thinking of a *specific* thing, or *any* thing?

</div>

---

# A vs An — The Sound Rule

Use **a** before consonant **sounds**, **an** before vowel **sounds**.

<v-clicks>

| Correct | Wrong | Why |
|---------|-------|-----|
| **a** university | ~~an university~~ | "university" starts with /j/ (y-sound) |
| **an** hour | ~~a hour~~ | "hour" starts with /aʊ/ (h is silent) |
| **an** MBA | ~~a MBA~~ | "M" is pronounced /em/ (vowel sound) |
| **a** European | ~~an European~~ | "European" starts with /j/ |

</v-clicks>

<div v-click class="mt-4 p-4 bg-blue-50 rounded-lg">

**Rule:** It's the **sound**, not the spelling.

</div>

# When to Use "The"

---

# Definite Article: "The"

Use `the` when:

<v-clicks>

| Situation | Example | Thai |
|-----------|---------|------|
| **Second mention** | "I saw a dog. **The** dog was big." | กล่าวถึงแล้ว |
| **Unique things** | "**The** sun", "**The** internet" | มีเพียงหนึ่งเดียว |
| **Shared knowledge** | "Close **the** door." | ทั้งสองรู้ว่าหมายถึงอะไร |
| **Superlatives** | "**The** best", "**The** tallest" | คำขั้นสุด |
| **Ordinal numbers** | "**The** first", "**The** second" | ลำดับที่ |
| **Specified by phrase** | "**The** man **in the blue shirt**" | มีคำขยาย |

</v-clicks>

---

# No Article (Zero Article)

Omit articles when:

<v-clicks>

| Situation | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| **General plural** | "Cats are independent." |
| **Uncountable (general)** | "Water is essential." |
| **Proper nouns** | "I live in Thailand." |
| **Meals** | "Let's have breakfast." |
| **Languages** | "She studies English." |
| **Sports** | "I play football." |

</v-clicks>

---

# Common Patterns

| Pattern | Use | Example |
|---------|-----|---------|
| `There is a/an...` | Introducing new | "There is **a** problem." |
| `It's the...` | Identifying specific | "It's **the** problem I mentioned." |
| `...of the...` | Almost always `the` | "**The** back of **the** room" |

# Common Mistakes

::left::

## Missing Articles

<v-clicks>

| Mistake | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| "I bought car." | "I bought **a** car." |
| "She is teacher." | "She is **a** teacher." |
| "Sun is hot." | "**The** sun is hot." |

**Why:** Thai has no articles. เธอเป็นครู — no word for "a".

</v-clicks>

::right::

## Over-correction

<v-clicks>

| Mistake | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| "I go to **the** school." | "I go to school." |
| "I like **the** music." | "I like music." |

**Why:** Adding "the" when you mean *in general*.

</v-clicks>

---

# Quick Test

Fill in `a`, `an`, `the`, or `—` (no article):

<v-clicks>

1. I need ___ new computer. -> **a**
2. She is ___ best student in class. -> **the**
3. ___ water in this glass is cold. -> **The**
4. He is ___ honest man. -> **an** (silent h)
5. I love ___ dogs. -> **—** (general plural)

</v-clicks>

# Key Takeaway

Thai has **no articles**. English uses them constantly.

The choice is: **specific** (the) vs **general** (a/an or nothing).
