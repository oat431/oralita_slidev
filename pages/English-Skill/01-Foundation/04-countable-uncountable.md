# Countable vs Uncountable

::left::

## Countable (นับได้)

- Can count: 1 apple, 2 apples
- Has **plural** form
- Use **a/an** with singular
- Quantifiers: **many**, **few**, **several**

::right::

## Uncountable (นับไม่ได้)

- Cannot count: ~~2 waters~~
- **No** plural form
- **No** a/an
- Quantifiers: **much**, **little**, **a bit of**

# Common Uncountable Nouns

<v-clicks>

| Category | Examples | Thai |
|----------|----------|------|
| **Liquids** | water, milk, coffee, oil | น้ำ นม กาแฟ น้ำมัน |
| **Gases** | air, oxygen, smoke | อากาศ ออกซิเจน ควัน |
| **Materials** | wood, metal, plastic, paper | ไม้ โลหะ พลาสติก กระดาษ |
| **Abstract** | love, happiness, information | ความรัก ความสุข ข้อมูล |
| **Subjects** | mathematics, physics | คณิตศาสตร์ ฟิสิกส์ |
| **Food (bulk)** | rice, sugar, flour, meat | ข้าว น้ำตาล แป้ง เนื้อ |
| **Languages** | English, Thai, Chinese | ภาษาอังกฤษ ภาษาไทย |

</v-clicks>

# Quantifiers: Which to Use

| Quantifier | Countable | Uncountable | Example |
|-----------|:---------:|:-----------:|---------|
| **many** | Yes | No | many books |
| **much** | No | Yes | much water |
| **a lot of** | Yes | Yes | a lot of books / water |
| **few / a few** | Yes | No | a few problems |
| **little / a little** | No | Yes | a little time |
| **some** | Yes | Yes | some books / water |
| **any** | Yes | Yes | any books? / water? |
| **several** | Yes | No | several ideas |
| **plenty of** | Yes | Yes | plenty of time / books |

# Few vs A Few, Little vs A Little

---

# Few vs A Few, Little vs A Little

| Expression | Meaning | Tone |
|-----------|---------|------|
| **a few** / **a little** | Some (enough) | Positive |
| **few** / **little** | Not enough (nearly none) | Negative |

<v-clicks>

<div class="grid grid-cols-2 gap-4 mt-4">
<div class="p-4 bg-green-50 rounded-lg">

**Positive:**
- "I have **a few** friends." — I'm okay
- "I have **a little** time." — enough

</div>
<div class="p-4 bg-red-50 rounded-lg">

**Negative:**
- "I have **few** friends." — almost none
- "I have **little** time." — nearly none

</div>
</div>

</v-clicks>

# Nouns That Can Be Both

| Noun | Uncountable | Countable |
|------|------------|-----------|
| **paper** | Material | A newspaper |
| **glass** | Material | A drinking glass |
| **hair** | All hair on head | A single strand |
| **time** | Duration | An occasion |
| **work** | Job, labor | A work of art |
| **experience** | Knowledge gained | An event |
| **chicken** | Food (meat) | The animal |

<v-clicks>

- "I ate **chicken**." — uncountable (meat)
- "I saw **a chicken**." — countable (animal)

</v-clicks>

# Making Uncountable Nouns Countable

Use "unit" words:

<v-clicks>

| Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|
| a **piece** of... | a piece of advice / furniture |
| a **glass** of... | a glass of water / milk |
| a **cup** of... | a cup of coffee / tea |
| a **slice** of... | a slice of bread / cake |
| a **bit** of... | a bit of information / luck |
| a **sheet** of... | a sheet of paper |
| a **bar** of... | a bar of chocolate / soap |

</v-clicks>

# Common Mistakes

::left::

## Direct Translation Errors

<v-clicks>

| Thai | Wrong | Correct |
|-------------|-------|---------|
| ขอน้ำสอง | two waters | two **glasses of** water |
| ฉันมีข้อมูล | many informations | a lot of **information** |
| เฟอร์นิเจอร์สามชิ้น | three furnitures | three **pieces of** furniture |

</v-clicks>

::right::

## Why This Happens

<div class="mt-4 p-4 bg-yellow-50 rounded-lg">

Thai uses **classifiers** differently. In English:

- Some nouns **cannot** be pluralized
- You need **unit words** (piece, glass, cup)
- The classifier system is **not the same**

</div>

---

# Quick Test

<v-clicks>

1. How ___ (many/much) money do you have? -> **much**
2. I have ___ (a few/a little) ideas. -> **a few**
3. She gave me good ___ (advice/advices). -> **advice**
4. There aren't ___ (many/much) chairs. -> **many**
5. I need ___ (a few/a little) help. -> **a little**

</v-clicks>

# Key Takeaway

English divides nouns into **countable** and **uncountable**.

This affects articles, quantifiers, and verb forms.

Thai doesn't make this distinction — it's a **new way of thinking**.
