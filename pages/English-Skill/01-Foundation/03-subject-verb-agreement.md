# The Basic Rule

| Subject | Verb Form | Example |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| Singular (he/she/it) | Verb + **s/es** | He **runs** every day. |
| Plural (they/we) | Base verb | They **run** every day. |
| I | Base verb (exception) | I **run** every day. |
| You | Base verb | You **run** every day. |

<div v-click class="mt-4 p-4 bg-yellow-50 rounded-lg">

**Think:** "He run**s**" — the **-s** on the verb matches the **singular** subject.

</div>

# Tricky Cases

---

# 1. Words Between Subject and Verb

The verb agrees with the **subject**, not the nearest noun.

<v-clicks>

| Correct | Wrong |
|---------|-------|
| The **box** of chocolates **is** expensive. | ~~The box of chocolates **are** expensive.~~ |
| The **students** in the class **are** tired. | ~~The students in the class **is** tired.~~ |

</v-clicks>

<div v-click class="mt-4 p-4 bg-red-50 rounded-lg">

**Trap:** "of chocolates" is just extra info. The subject is **box** (singular).

</div>

---

# 2. Either/Or and Neither/Nor

The verb agrees with the **closest** subject.

<v-clicks>

| Rule | Example |
|------|---------|
| Either A **or** B -> verb with **B** | Either the teacher or the **students are** responsible. |
| Neither A **nor** B -> verb with **B** | Neither the students nor the **teacher is** responsible. |

</v-clicks>

---

# 3. Indefinite Pronouns

| Always Singular | Always Plural | Depends |
|----------------|---------------|---------|
| everyone, someone, anyone | both, few, many, several | all, some, none, most |
| each, every, either, neither | | |
| nobody, nothing, no one | | |

<v-clicks>

- **Everybody loves** pizza. (singular)
- **Both are** correct. (plural)
- **Some of the cake is** gone. / **Some of the cookies are** gone.

</v-clicks>

---

# 4. Collective Nouns

| Singular (one unit) | Plural (individuals) |
|------------------------------|-------------------------------------|
| The **team is** winning. | The **team are** arguing. |
| The **family is** large. | The **family are** going separate ways. |

<div v-click class="mt-4 p-4 bg-blue-50 rounded-lg">

**Note:** American English prefers **singular**. British English uses both.

</div>

---

# 5. There is / There are

The subject comes **after** the verb.

<v-clicks>

| Correct | Wrong |
|---------|-------|
| There **is** a **book** on the table. | ~~There are a book.~~ |
| There **are** three **books** on the table. | ~~There is three books.~~ |

</v-clicks>

# Why This Is Hard for Thai

Thai verbs **don't change form** based on subject.

<v-clicks>

| Thai | Wrong | Correct |
|-------------|-------|---------|
| เขาไปโรงเรียน | He go to school. | He **goes** to school. |
| ฉันชอบหมา | I likes dogs. | I **like** dogs. |
| พวกเขาวิ่ง | They runs fast. | They **run** fast. |

</v-clicks>

::right::

<div class="mt-12 p-4 bg-yellow-50 rounded-lg">

**Remember:**

- he/she/it -> verb **+s**
- I/you/we/they -> verb **no change**
- The verb changes, not the subject!

</div>

---

# Quick Test

<v-clicks>

1. Everyone ___ (is/are) welcome. -> **is**
2. The list of items ___ (is/are) long. -> **is**
3. Neither the cat nor the dogs ___ (was/were) fed. -> **were**
4. There ___ (is/are) many problems. -> **are**
5. The team ___ (has/have) decided. -> **has** (AmE) / **have** (BrE)

</v-clicks>

---
layout: center
---

# Key Takeaway

In English, the verb **changes form** to match the subject.

In Thai, it doesn't. This is a **new habit** to build.
