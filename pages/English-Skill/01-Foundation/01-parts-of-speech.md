# The Eight Parts of Speech

---

# The Eight Parts

| # | Part | Job | Example | Thai |
|---|------|-----|---------|------|
| 1 | **Noun** | Names things | dog, Bangkok | คำนาม |
| 2 | **Pronoun** | Replaces a noun | I, you, he, she | สรรพนาม |
| 3 | **Verb** | Shows action/state | run, eat, is | คำกริยา |
| 4 | **Adjective** | Describes a noun | big, red, happy | คำคุณศัพท์ |
| 5 | **Adverb** | Describes a verb | quickly, always | คำกริยาวิเศษณ์ |
| 6 | **Preposition** | Shows relationship | in, on, at, to | คำบุพบท |
| 7 | **Conjunction** | Connects things | and, but, because | คำสันธาน |
| 8 | **Interjection** | Expresses emotion | Wow! Ouch! | คำอุทาน |

---

# 1. Nouns — Types

<v-clicks>

| Type | Example | Notes |
|------|---------|-------|
| **Common** | city, book | General things |
| **Proper** | London, Harry Potter | Specific names — always capitalized |
| **Abstract** | love, happiness | Ideas, feelings — can't touch |
| **Concrete** | table, water | Physical things — can touch |
| **Collective** | team, family | Group treated as one unit |

</v-clicks>

---

# 2. Pronouns — Types

| Type | Examples | Replaces... |
|------|----------|------------|
| **Subject** | I, you, he, she, it, we, they | Who does the action |
| **Object** | me, you, him, her, it, us, them | Who receives the action |
| **Possessive** | my, your, his, her, its, our, their | Who owns something |
| **Reflexive** | myself, yourself, himself | Subject = object |
| **Demonstrative** | this, that, these, those | Pointing to something |

---

# 3. Verbs — Types

<v-clicks>

| Type | Example | Meaning |
|------|---------|---------|
| **Action** | run, write, build | Physical or mental action |
| **Linking** | is, seems, becomes | Connects subject to description |
| **Helping** | is, have, do, will, can | Helps main verb form tense/mood |
| **Transitive** | She ate **an apple** | Needs an object |
| **Intransitive** | She **sleeps** | No object needed |

</v-clicks>

# 4. Adjectives & 5. Adverbs

::left::

## Adjectives

Describe **nouns**

Answer: *Which one? What kind? How many?*

**Order matters:**

Opinion -> Size -> Age -> Shape -> Color -> Origin -> Material -> Noun

> "A **beautiful small old round red Italian wooden** table"

::right::

## Adverbs

Describe **verbs**, adjectives, or adverbs

Often end in `-ly`

| Type | Example |
|------|---------|
| Manner | quickly, carefully |
| Time | yesterday, soon |
| Place | here, everywhere |
| Frequency | always, sometimes |
| Degree | very, extremely |

---

# 6, 7, 8 — Quick Overview

<v-clicks>

### 6. Prepositions
Show relationships in **time**, **place**, and **direction**
- Time: `at 5pm`, `on Monday`, `in June`
- Place: `at the door`, `on the table`, `in the room`
- Direction: `to school`, `from Bangkok`

### 7. Conjunctions
Connect words, phrases, or clauses
- FANBOYS: `for, and, nor, but, or, yet, so`
- Subordinating: `because, although, if`

### 8. Interjections
Standalone emotion words: `Wow!`, `Oh no!`, `Oops!`, `Yay!`

</v-clicks>

# Quick Test

Identify the part of speech for each **bold** word:

> The **happy** dog **quickly** ran **to** the park **and** played **with** his ball.

<v-clicks>

| Word | Part of Speech |
|------|---------------|
| **happy** | Adjective (describes "dog") |
| **quickly** | Adverb (describes "ran") |
| **to** | Preposition (direction) |
| **and** | Conjunction (connects two verbs) |
| **with** | Preposition (relationship) |

</v-clicks>

# Key Takeaway

Every word in a sentence has a **job**.

Knowing the eight parts of speech is the foundation of all English grammar.
